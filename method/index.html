Under the hood

The instruments behind the work.

Two field-tested instruments do the measuring. Here is what each one looks at, in plain language.

Business Assessment

We map the whole company across eight areas and score each one on the same rubric. Behind the score is a failure library of 150+ ways a business breaks. We rate each by how likely it is and what it would cost you, then rank them so you seal the most likely, most expensive leak first.

One readiness score. You get one overall readiness score out of 100, plus a score for each of the eight areas, so you can see at a glance where the business is strong and where it is thin.

Formally the Business Architecture & Intelligence Cycle (BAIC).

Leadership DNA

We score each leader twice: how they are naturally wired, and what their seat actually demands. The gap between the two is the daily energy they burn to fit the role, and the coaching that would relieve it. It is a developmental read, never a hiring or selection screen.

Scored inside the system. Leadership DNA is scored as part of the business diagnosis, not as a separate personality test.

Formally Leadership Drivers & Natural Aptitudes (LDNA).

The interlock

You cannot score the business without reading the leaders inside it.

Leadership DNA is a scored input to how the company fails, feeding the leadership-failure-risk domain of the business diagnosis directly. That takes both instruments and a failure-analysis background at once. It is why a complete assessment includes both Leadership DNA and the Business Assessment, and why leadership-only work always points back to the full picture.
