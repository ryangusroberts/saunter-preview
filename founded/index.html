Most early ventures do not fail from a bad idea. They fail from a handful of preventable gaps, usually the right things done in the wrong order. FOUNDED is the map: the exact sequence that carries you from an idea to real, paying traction, and the failure each step is built to prevent.

The sequence is the method

Six stages, in the order that keeps you alive.

Most early failure comes from running these out of order: building before validating, scaling before proving, marketing before there is anything worth marketing. Each stage exists to defuse a specific, high-prevalence way early companies fall apart.

01

Foundation

Get honest about you, your runway, and your reasons before spending a dollar.

02

Problem & Market

Find a real problem worth solving, and the specific person who has it.

03

Validation

Prove demand before you build. Learn to hear truth, not praise.

04

Offer & Model

Turn validated demand into an offer that can actually make money.

05

Proof & First Revenue

Earn real, paying traction before you pour on fuel.

06

Foundation for Growth

Make sure the foundation can carry the weight before you add it.
