The insight that changes decisions

It is not who is good or bad. It is who fits the seat.

A strong leader in a role their wiring fights looks like a performance problem. Usually it is a fit problem, and once you can see it, it is fixable without losing the person.

The cost of not naming it

A miscast executive does not just underperform. They tax everyone around them.

Replacing a wrong-seat leader runs roughly 1.5 to 2x their salary (Source: SHRM). Leaving them there costs more: missed deadlines, a team quietly adjusting around the friction, and your strongest people starting to look. Naming it early is the cheapest move you have.
